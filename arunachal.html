Dirang

Dirang, 42 km from Bomdila, is a small town on the banks of River Kameng. Dirang lies on the road to Tawng and a popular place for tourists to halt here for a night on their way to Tawang as the journey from the border of Assam to Tawang is a long and arduous one and not possible for most tourist to cover in one day. In other words Dirang serves as the gateway to the city of Tawang. It is famous for its Monasteries, Hot water springs, Apple and Kiwi orchards, National Yak Research and Breeding Centre at Nigmadung, Sheep Breeding Farm. If you are someone who loves the wilderness, you should definitely stay for a night in Sangti Valley in Dirang and explore the heavenly valley.

With the spectacular landscape pleasing your eyes and the pleasant weather throughout the year, Dirang is a must-visit for every tourist looking to unwind in gorgeous surroundings. The quaint hill town with its scenic beauty and smiling people makes Dirang a paradise for nature lovers, photographers and trekkers.

Se-La

A breathtaking place in Arunachal Pradesh is a high altitude mountain pass Se-La, with La denoting Pass is a must visit attraction. Located in Tawang District of Arunachal Pradesh Sela Pass sits at an elevation of 4,170 meters and you can experience a slice of heaven on earth here. It's cool environs and adrenaline pumping height is simply spellbinding. The pass lies on the road from Bomdila to Tawang and tourists can't say no to a few moments of stoppage here and admire the breathtaking view and vibe of Sela.

While Sela Pass does get heavy snow during winters, there is no stopping to experiencing that either. It is usually open throughout the year unless landslides slide in of course. An interesting feature of Sela Pass is that, this place houses around 101 lakes around the pass. One such spectacular and famous lakes in Arunachal Pradesh is Sela Lake, it is also called as a Paradise Lake.

Tawang

Tawang is a district in Arunachal Pradesh sitting at a height of 3025 metres above sea level. Tawang shares it's border with Bhutan and Tibet. Tawang has been a bone of contention between India and China for quite a few decades now. The natural beauty and solitude of Gudpi and Chong-Chugmi ranges, Tawang Chu River and Tawang Valley are very mesmerising. There are many beautiful glacial lakes in and around Tawang with crystal blue waters such as Sela Lake, P.T.Tso Lake, Sangester Lake, Banggachang Lake and many more. These remain frozen in winters while in summers they become a haven for migratory birds. You can also visit Bum-la Pass which lies on the Indo-China border for which prior permit and vehicles are required.

The inhabitants of the districts are all of Monpa tribes. There are two major religious festivals of the Monpas, Losar and Torgya. Both festivals are celebrated annually. Losar is celebrated for the commencement of the New Year. Every third year of Torgya, the festival of Dungyur is celebrated. Both Dungyur and Torgya festivals are celebrated at the premises of the Tawang Monastery with traditional gaiety and enthusiasm.

Tawang is known for many important and beautiful monasteries and is famous for being the birthplace of the 6th Dalai Lama, Tsangyang Gyatso.

Mechuka

Mechuka or Menchukha, is a small town in Arunachal Pradesh that lies 6,000 ft above sea level, surrounded by pine trees and bushes. It is one of the lesser explored valleys in this Himalayan region and thus unspoilt. A surreal valley with undulating meadows where horses run wild, sums up the dreamscape of Mechuka valley. This remote valley, 29 kilometres from the China border is one of the most beautiful and unspoilt areas in India. This beauty is further accentuated by the local Memba tribes who reside in quaint wooden houses besides their millet and buckwheat fields. Not only is Mechuka visually stunning, it promises to enrich you with a cultural experience like never before. Memories of your homestay here will stay with you forever, that’s a promise!

The main attraction is the 400-year-old Samten Yongcha Monastery of Mahayana Buddhist sect. It houses numerous ancient statues, including that of Guru Padmasambhava, considered to be founder of the Nyingma sect. Colourful costumes and masks created for folklore figures found in Tibetan mythology can also be seen. These masks are often worn during a traditional dance called cham, which is an energetic and lively event that teaches people to shun all that is dark within them and embrace the good tenets of life according to Buddhism, like compassion and love. Many Tibetan festivals, especially Losar, are celebrated with reverence. The Losar festival, celebrated to mark the auspicious start of a new year, sees a lot of visitors who come to offer prayers.

Mechuka known for its pristine beauty, cultural tribes and gushing rivers that allow for sports like kayaking and rafting. The Siang river, which originates from the glaciers around the Mansarovar Lake in Tibet, flows through Mechuka and provides a breathtaking view of the valley. There are many bamboo walking bridges that connect the banks of this river. Walking on these gently swaying bridges, while the water gushes below, can be quite thrilling. Terraced rice fields, dotted with small farms, can be seen on the slopes of the valleys in this agrarian belt.

Dense virgin forests, spectacular snow capped mountains and gushing rivers cutting deep gorges, few other places in the world come close to the raw, rugged beauty of Arunachal Pradesh. The remoteness of the valleys has helped in preserving to a great extent the rich culture of the 26 plus tribes residing in its verdant slopes. And we invite you to experience this very diverse beauty first hand in Mechuka Valley.
